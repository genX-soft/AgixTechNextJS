## What Is an AI Voice Agent? `[LT: what is an ai voice agent]`

An AI voice agent `[PK]` is an intelligent phone system powered by speech recognition, large language models, and natural text-to-speech synthesis that can hold real-time, dynamic conversations with callers — understanding context, asking follow-up questions, making decisions, and executing actions across business systems during the call.

Unlike traditional IVR systems that force callers through rigid menu trees ("Press 1 for sales, press 2 for support"), AI voice agents `[PK]` engage in natural, flowing dialogue. A caller can say "I need to reschedule my appointment for next Tuesday afternoon" and the voice agent understands the request, checks calendar availability, offers options, confirms the change, and sends a confirmation — all within a single, natural conversation.

The technology stack behind a modern AI voice agent `[PK]` includes four core components working in real-time:

1. **Speech-to-Text (STT)** — converts the caller's spoken words into text in milliseconds (`[TOOL]` Deepgram provides sub-300ms transcription with accent handling and noise robustness)
2. **Large Language Model (LLM)** — interprets intent, generates contextual responses, makes decisions (`[TOOL]` OpenAI GPT-4o, `[TOOL]` Anthropic Claude, or `[TOOL]` Google Gemini depending on use case)
3. **Text-to-Speech (TTS)** — converts the AI's text response back into natural, human-sounding speech (`[TOOL]` Cartesia Sonic 3 delivers sub-100ms latency for conversational rhythm)
4. **Telephony & Orchestration** — manages the call routing, recording, and integration with business systems (`[TOOL]` Vapi for orchestration, `[TOOL]` Twilio for telephony infrastructure)
