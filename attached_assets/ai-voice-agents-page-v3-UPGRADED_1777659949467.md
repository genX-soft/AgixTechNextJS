## Technical Note: Why Latency Matters

*Voice agents must respond within 300–500ms to feel natural:*

- **Streaming STT** — transcription during speech, not after (`[TOOL]` Deepgram)
- **Low-latency LLM** — response in <200ms (`[TOOL]` GPT-4o mini for speed)
- **Real-time TTS** — `[TOOL]` Cartesia Sonic 3 generates speech in <100ms

`[CITE]` Systems >800ms feel robotic, reduce trust (CB Insights). Most demos run 1–2s — fine for demos, unacceptable in production. AGIX targets sub-400ms measured end-to-end.

## Where Voice Agents Fail

**1. Poor conversation design** — rigid scripts fail on variations. AGIX tests 100+ real scenarios.
**2. High latency (>800ms)** — feels robotic, callers hang up. AGIX targets sub-400ms.
**3. Weak intent detection** — "cancel" vs "reschedule" errors erode trust. Multi-model verification: <3% misclassification.
**4. No fallback** — silent confusion destroys experience. Must transfer to human with context.
**5. Missing post-call integration** — perfect call but no CRM update = more work. [Automation](/services/ai-automation/) handles execution.

**`[ORIGINAL]` Voice AI fails when treated as standalone script. Succeeds as the interaction layer of a connected system.`**
