## What Is AI Route Optimization?

*AI route optimization uses machine learning to calculate the most efficient delivery routes in real time — factoring in traffic conditions, weather, delivery time windows, vehicle capacity, driver schedules, and fuel costs. Unlike static route planning (fixed routes updated manually), AI reroutes dynamically as conditions change throughout the day.*

## How AI Works in Logistics — Simplified
1. **Signal collection** — GPS, IoT sensors, order data, weather feeds, traffic APIs, inventory levels
2. **Pattern analysis** — AI identifies demand patterns, route inefficiencies, delay root causes
3. **Prediction** — demand forecasts, ETAs, maintenance needs, capacity requirements
4. **Optimization** — route planning, load balancing, warehouse task sequencing, inventory positioning
5. **Execution** — automated dispatch, dynamic rerouting, inventory rebalancing via [AI Automation](/services/ai-automation/)

## AI Governance in Logistics: Reliability & Override

| Principle | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Decision override** | Dispatchers override any AI routing/scheduling decision instantly |
| **Confidence thresholds** | AI flags low-confidence predictions for human review |
| **Fail-safe design** | System failures default to last-known-good routes, not empty screens |
| **Data accuracy** | Sensor calibration monitoring is continuous — AI is only as good as its data |
| **Audit trails** | Every routing decision, ETA, and inventory action logged with reasoning |
| **SLA accountability** | AI decisions tied to delivery SLA performance — measured, not assumed |

## Limitations of AI in Logistics

**1. Data quality determines prediction quality.** IoT sensors, GPS, and inventory systems with accuracy gaps produce unreliable outputs.
**2. Unprecedented disruptions break models.** Pandemics, port closures, extreme weather without precedent confuse pattern-based models.
**3. Model drift is silent.** Routes, demand patterns change gradually. Models degrade without continuous monitoring.
**4. Over-optimization creates fragility.** Zero-buffer supply chains optimized for maximum efficiency are most vulnerable to disruption.
