## Limitations of AI in Insurance

**1. Biased training data → biased underwriting.** Historical claims and pricing data can encode systemic discrimination. Continuous fairness auditing across protected attributes is non-negotiable — not a one-time test.

**2. Fraud detection generates false positives.** Legitimate claims flagged as fraud damage customer relationships and trust. Threshold tuning and human review balance security with service quality.

**3. Model drift degrades accuracy silently.** Claim patterns, fraud tactics, and risk profiles evolve continuously. Models without automated retraining pipelines lose accuracy over months — often without anyone noticing until audit.

**4. Regulatory complexity varies by jurisdiction.** AI that's compliant in one state or country may not be in another. Multi-jurisdictional architecture and governance flexibility are essential.

**`[ORIGINAL]` AI in insurance must be more than accurate — it must be provably fair, consistently explainable, and continuously audited. Regulators don't accept "the model said so" as justification for any decision.`**

## Frequently Asked Questions (12)

### How is AI used in insurance?
AI automates claims processing, detects fraud in real time, accelerates underwriting, extracts data from documents, improves customer experience through conversational AI, and enables dynamic risk-based pricing — across all insurance lines.

### What is AI claims automation?
AI claims automation uses ML to triage claims by complexity, auto-adjudicate routine claims within governance rules, and route complex cases to human adjusters with AI-generated summaries, damage assessments, and fraud probability scores.

### Can AI process claims automatically?
Yes — for routine claims. AI handles the 60–70% of claims that follow standard patterns. Complex, high-value, or suspicious claims always go to human adjusters with full AI-generated context.

### Is insurance AI regulatory-compliant?
All AGIX systems include explainable decisions, complete audit trails, continuous bias monitoring, and governance aligned with state/federal regulations and NAIC guidelines.

### How does AI detect insurance fraud?
ML models analyze claim patterns, claimant behavior histories, provider network relationships, and known fraud typologies. Network analysis identifies organized rings. Flagging happens at submission — not post-payment.

### What about AI bias in underwriting?
AGIX builds continuous fairness auditing into every underwriting and pricing model. Demographic, geographic, and socioeconomic bias is monitored in production — not just tested once pre-deployment.

### What should insurance companies implement first?
Claims automation or document intelligence — fastest ROI and most measurable impact. Both deliver results within 6–9 weeks. Scale to fraud detection and underwriting after.

### What are the limitations of AI in insurance?
Bias risk in historical training data, false positives in fraud detection, silent model drift, and multi-jurisdictional regulatory complexity. Architecture and governance prevent these from becoming operational failures.
